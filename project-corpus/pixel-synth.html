Problem

Turning visual ideas into sound composition typically requires multiple tools. I wanted a playful, immediate way to compose by painting—where colors, shapes, and density translate into timbre, pitch, and rhythm.

Approach

PixelSynth maps a canvas grid to a step sequencer. Each pixel row is a pitch, columns are time slices, and color hue maps to instrument/timbre. Saturation drives filter cutoff and brightness drives amplitude envelopes.

Sound Architecture

// Pseudocode
engine = AudioContext()
bus = engine.createGain()
seq = new Sequencer(steps=64, tracks=16)
for track in seq.tracks:
  synth = createSynth(type=track.hue)
  fx = [compressor(), delay(tempoSync), reverb(room=0.3)]
  chain = connect(synth, ...fx, bus)
  track.output = chain
bus.connect(engine.destination)

Interactions

  • Paint modes: pen, line, fill, eraser.
  • Live performance: mute/solo tracks, tempo nudge, swing, snapshot recall.
  • Export: render stems or master mix to WAV.

Outcomes

Prototype produces musical loops reliably at 120 BPM with <10ms scheduling jitter. Early testers report it “feels like playing an instrument” rather than drawing notes.
